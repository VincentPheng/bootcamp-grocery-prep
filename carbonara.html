Simple Man's Carbonara

So good, it'll make the italians weep

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp salt
  • 4 oz. guanciale/pancetta/bacon
  • 2 oz. parmesean
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 large eggs
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 lb. spaghetti or noodle of your choice

Instructions

  1. Heat a large pot of water until steaming, then add the salt. Continue heating until the water boils
  2. While waiting for the water to come to a boil, chop up your pork meat of choice into short strips
  3. Finely grate the parmesean. Set aside about a quarter of the cheese for later
  4. Whisk the egg yolks and whole eggs until no egg white streaks remain. Stir in the remaining grated cheese. Add pepper to the egg mixture to your tastes
  5. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium then add the meat, stirring occasionally until crisp
  6. Fish out the meat with a wooden spoon and transfer to a small bowl. Discard most of the fat, leaving only a thin layer at the bottom of the pot
  7. Drop in the pasta into the bowling water and, just before the pasta is done cooking according to the instrcutions, scoop out about a cup of the pasta water for later
  8. Drain the pasta and add it to the pot with fat from earlier, cooking over medium-high heat
  9. Keep cooking the pasta, stirring and occasionally for about 2 minutes
  10. Slowly drizzle in the egg mixture from earlier. Make sure you continue stirring constantly until the pasta thickens up
  11. If the pasta becomes too thick, you can thin it out with the pasta water from earlier.
  12. Stir in the pork and plate up. Add anymore pepper and the parmesean you saved earlier.
