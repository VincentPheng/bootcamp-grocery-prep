Pineapple Fried Rice

The simple people pleaser dish gets an acidic upgrade that'll make you forget the 3 bowls you already ate

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or a any neutral flavored oil
  • 2 eggs, beaten with salt
  • 1 1/2 cups of chopped pineapple
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, pressed or minced
  • 1-2 tsp sriracha
  • 2 cups of cooked rice
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 small lime, halved
  • Salt, to taste
  • Cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat a large frying pan over medium high heat until it starts to steam. Add a small amount of oil along with the eggs and begin scrambling for about a minute. Transfer the eggs to a bowl and clean the pan
  2. Add more oil into the pan and cook the pineapple and bell pepper, stirring constantly until the pineapple is caramelized. Add green onions and garlic and continue to cook until fragrant. Add contents to your bowl of eggs
  3. Reduce the heat to medium and add the rest of the oil. Add the rice and continue cooking until it's hot, about 3 minutes
  4. Pour the contents of the egg bowl into the pan and stir to combine with the rice. Cook until the contents are hot then turn off the heat. Add soy sauce, sriracha, and the squeeze of 1/2 a lime. Season to taste and set aside.
  5. Slice the remaining half of the lime into 4 wedges and plate up the rice, serving with each plate a wedge of lime.
