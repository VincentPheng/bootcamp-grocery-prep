Cantonese Style Scrambled Eggs

Fool proof way to never eat rubber eggs ever again

Note: This recipe contains some strongly flavored asian ingrdients, namely the wine and sesame oil.
If you don't like the taste of them or don't have them available, simply omit them from the recipe

Ingredients

  • 5 eggs
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/8 tsp white pepper
  • 1/8 tsp MSG/Chicken boullion powder
  • (Optional)1/2 tsp Shaoxing Wine
  • 1tsp cornstarch
  • 1tbsp water
  • 1 tsp sesame oil or lard (Neutral flavored oil such as vegetable oil will also work)
  • (Optional) 1/2 lb. of shrimp, cooked

Instructions

  1. Crack the five eggs into a bowl and whisk until you see large bubbles form
  2. Combine the cornstarch and water to make a cornstarch slurry and add that to the egg mixture
  3. Add the rest of the seasonings into the egg mixture and whisk until you can see bubbles again
  4. Add the shrimp at this step as well if you have any.
  5. Heat the sesame oil or lard in a non stick skillet until it starts steaming, then add the eggs. After this you must act quickly as the intense heat will quickly set the eggs
  6. When the outer edges begin to set, pull the cooked bits to one side of the skillet. Now move the skillet so that the uncooked part is over the flame.
  7. Again, when it begins to set, layer the newly cooked sided up and over the the previous layer
  8. Continue this process until all the egg is set and cooked. If the pan starts getting too hot, feel free to take it off the heat for a moment to let it cool down before continuing
  9. Cut the finshed scrambled egg in half and stack them on top of each other. Garnish with scallions if available
